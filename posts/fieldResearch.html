These two are Valve and Stress Level Zero. They both started as game developement companies. After valve created steam, they branched out towards other branches like publishing and hosting their own digital distribution platform, named steam. They both have characterstics which I would describe as befitting my own view on what kind of company I would like to work in or do an internship in. One reason is the freedom they have in their work, namely valve. They have wheels under their desks so that they may move over to whatever project they feel like working on. This is a testament to their freedom. They can have this freedom only because of their lucrative platform steam is raising enough funds for them to experiment and make mistakes. This freedom however does come at a cost. A phenomena has been named after the way in which valve decides to release their products, it's called "valve time". Because valve has this freedom, they can do whatever they want. Even if it's a non realistic idea which is likely to never be published. But on the other side of the coin, because they have this freedom, everything they make is of the highest quality. You can really feel the effort and passion that goes into what they make. After completion of their games, you unlock the developer commentary. In which the developers themselves go into the thought process of how a peticulair section of the game is designed, what problems they faced and the solutions they came up with and how the play testers responded to them.

Stress Level Zero is a VR game developer. They are the most known for making boneworks. Their games can be better classified as physics simulations. their aim is to create a truly believable world which acts and responds in the same manner our own would do. from everything like interacting with objects in a believable way to inserting your own body size so your real life hands line up with your ingame avatar. Making it all the more believable. They are a small group of 20 people and I would like to work like that as well. In an intimate group of coleagues who you can trust and rely upon. I don't think I'll end up becoming a game developer, but the way these two companies work is applicable to all companies. In my work I'd like to have freedom (to a limited (but acceptable) degree), have a healthy work environment and coleagues of the same mind.
